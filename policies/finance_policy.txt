FINANCE DEPARTMENT POLICIES
============================

EXPENSE MANAGEMENT AND REIMBURSEMENT
1. All business expenses must be pre-approved by the employee's direct manager before being incurred.
2. Expense reimbursement claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense date; claims beyond this window will not be processed.
3. Original receipts or digital invoices are mandatory for all reimbursements above INR 500 (or local currency equivalent).
4. Employees must use the official expense management portal (expenses.company.com) to submit claims; paper submissions are not accepted.
5. Travel expenses for domestic trips are subject to a per-diem allowance defined in the Travel Policy Matrix and reviewed annually.

PROCUREMENT AND VENDOR MANAGEMENT
6. Any purchase above INR 50,000 (or equivalent) requires a formal Purchase Order (PO) raised through the ERP system and approved by Finance.
7. Vendor empanelment must follow the standard due diligence process, including legal and compliance review, before any contract is signed.
8. Employees must not enter into verbal agreements or commit company funds without a signed contract and Finance approval.
9. Competitive bids (minimum 3 vendors) are required for purchases above INR 1,00,000; single-vendor justification must be documented.
10. All vendor invoices must be submitted to accounts@company.com within 5 business days of service delivery.

BUDGET PLANNING AND CONTROL
11. Annual budgets are planned in Q4 for the following financial year; department heads must submit budget proposals by November 30.
12. Mid-year budget revisions require a formal request to Finance with justification and CFO approval.
13. Departments must not overspend their approved budgets without written Finance authorization; unauthorized overspending is a policy violation.
14. Monthly budget utilization reports are shared by Finance with all department heads by the 5th of each month.
15. Surplus budget cannot be transferred between departments without CFO approval.

FINANCIAL COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT
16. All financial transactions must comply with applicable tax laws, accounting standards (e.g., IFRS/GAAP), and regulatory requirements.
17. Employees must cooperate fully with internal and external audits; withholding financial information from auditors is a serious violation.
18. Internal audits are conducted quarterly; findings are reported to the Audit Committee within 15 business days.
19. Any suspected financial fraud, misappropriation, or irregularity must be reported immediately to the Finance Controller or via the anonymous whistleblower portal.
20. Financial records must be retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with statutory requirements.

PAYROLL AND SALARY ADMINISTRATION
21. Salaries are disbursed on the last working day of each month directly to the employee's registered bank account.
22. Any change in bank account details must be submitted to payroll@company.com at least 10 business days before the payroll processing date.
23. Payslips are generated digitally and accessible via the employee self-service portal by the 1st of each month.
24. Tax declarations (investments, deductions) must be submitted by January 15 each year to avoid excess TDS deductions.
25. Loan or advance requests against salary are permissible up to a maximum of 2 months' gross salary and require Finance and HR approval.

TRAVEL AND ACCOMMODATION
26. Business travel must be booked through the approved corporate travel desk; personal bookings require prior Finance approval for reimbursement eligibility.
27. Economy class travel is the standard for domestic trips; business class is permitted only for international trips above 6 hours with CFO approval.
28. Hotel accommodation is covered up to the approved grade limit as per the Travel Policy Matrix; upgrades are at the employee's own expense.
29. All travel advances must be settled within 7 days of return by submitting an expense report with receipts.
